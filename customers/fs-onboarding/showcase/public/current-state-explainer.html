Systemic Findings

What the Process Flow Reveals

Stepping back from individual phases, the end-to-end flow exposes structural patterns that no single team can solve alone.

🗺
No Documented Process Baseline
The end-to-end process flow is not clearly documented, making it difficult to understand how work progresses from intake to activation. Teams operate on tribal knowledge.
👥
Ambiguous Roles & Ownership
Roles and responsibilities are inconsistently defined, creating ambiguity around step-level ownership and overall accountability. No single guided, accountable owner for the end-to-end process.
🔀
Informal Handoffs & Re-routing
Work moves through informal handoffs and re-routing rather than a standardized, traceable flow. Teams rely on "phone a friend" to navigate approvals and processes.
🏛
Reinforced Functional Silos
The lack of a single accountable owner reinforces functional silos, prompting teams to rely on who they know to navigate approvals rather than a defined, repeatable process.
Execution Impact

Timeline & Execution Implications

These structural issues translate directly into measurable execution problems that affect every request flowing through the system.

📊
Timelines Impossible to Analyze
Current-state timelines are difficult to analyze due to the lack of a documented process baseline. Without measurement, improvement targets remain arbitrary.
🔄
Complex Back-and-Forth
Flow is complex with frequent back-and-forth between teams and repeated touchpoints. A single request may bounce between the same groups multiple times before resolution.
🚧
Rework from Unclear Criteria
Rework and delays are driven by unclear entry/exit criteria at each step, insufficient information, and lack of following the existing processes that do exist.
🎲
Unpredictable Execution Paths
Inconsistent execution paths create unpredictable cycle times, even for similar requests. Two identical vendor onboardings can take dramatically different amounts of time.
