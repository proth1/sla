Deal-Killer Screen

If ANY of these are "No", the request cannot proceed to the pipeline. Discuss with the requester first.
No budget = no path forward. Requester must secure at minimum a preliminary funding indication from their cost center owner.
Every request must have a named individual accountable for business outcomes. "The team wants it" is not sponsorship.
Check the software portfolio register. If a similar capability exists, route to portfolio management instead.
"Nice to have" requests without strategic alignment get deprioritized indefinitely. Confirm alignment before consuming reviewer bandwidth.
Requests with impossible timelines create false urgency and undermine governance. If truly urgent, route to the emergency/forced-update path.
!!
If any item above is unchecked: Do NOT enter this into the pipeline. Return to the requester with specific guidance on what they need to provide. Use the "Pre-Screen Rejection" email template below.

Risk Tier Classification Worksheet

Score the request to determine Fast-Track, Standard, or Enhanced review. This replaces the DMN_RiskTierClassification decision table for manual use.

Scoring Matrix

Score each dimension 1-3. Total determines the tier.
Dimension 1 - Low 2 - Medium 3 - High Score
Data Sensitivity No PII/PHI, public data only Internal data, limited PII Regulated data (PII/PHI/SOX/financial)
Vendor Criticality Existing vendor, proven track record Known vendor, new product/module New vendor, no prior relationship
Financial Exposure Under $50K annual $50K - $500K annual Over $500K annual
Integration Complexity Standalone SaaS, no integrations API integration with non-critical systems Integrates with core banking / payment systems
AI / ML Usage No AI components AI features (recommendations, analytics) AI in decision-making (credit, fraud, compliance)
Regulatory Exposure No regulatory requirements Standard regulatory (GDPR, SOX) Multi-regulation (OCC, DORA, EU AI Act, SR 11-7)
Total Score: --

Tier Routing Guide

Based on total score, assign the appropriate review level.
Score Tier Reviewers Required Target SLA Escalation Path
6-8 Fast-Track Quarterback + 1 reviewer (Security or Compliance) 5 business days Quarterback's manager after Day 3
9-13 Standard 4 reviewers in parallel (Security, Compliance, Legal, Tech Architecture) 15 business days Governance lead after Day 10
14-18 Enhanced All 9 reviewers in parallel + executive briefing 25 business days CISO/CRO after Day 15
Override Rule: If ANY single dimension scores a 3, the minimum tier is Standard regardless of total. A 3 in AI/ML + regulatory automatically escalates to Enhanced.

Weekly Governance Stand-Up

30-minute maximum. Run every week at the same time. The Quarterback facilitates. No deep dives — status and decisions only.

Stand-Up Agenda

Time-boxed: 30 minutes total. Print this with the tracker filled in.
Time Box Agenda Item Description Owner
2 min Pipeline Summary How many requests total? How many in each phase? How many breached SLA? Quarterback
10 min SLA Breaches Walk through EACH breached request. What is blocking? Who needs to act? Decision: escalate, extend, or kill? Quarterback + Blockers
8 min At-Risk Requests Requests approaching SLA deadline (within 2 business days). What's needed to close on time? Quarterback + Reviewers
5 min New Intake Any new requests entering the pipeline this week? Quick tier assignment and reviewer identification. Quarterback
5 min Decisions Needed Any go/no-go decisions the committee needs to make right now? Governance approvals waiting? Governance Lead
i
Ground Rules: No deep-dive discussions. If a topic needs more than 2 minutes, schedule a separate meeting. The Quarterback takes notes and publishes action items within 1 hour of the meeting.

SLA Escalation Playbook

When a review or phase exceeds its SLA target, follow this escalation ladder. The Quarterback owns escalation — do not wait for someone else to notice.

Escalation Ladder by Phase

Each phase has its own SLA target and escalation path. Times are cumulative from phase start.
Phase SLA Target Day Warning Sent Escalate To (Level 1) Escalate To (Level 2)
Intake / Triage 2 business days Day 1 (50%) Quarterback's manager (Day 2) Governance Lead (Day 3)
Planning 5 business days Day 3 (60%) Governance Lead (Day 5) CRO (Day 7)
Due Diligence 10 business days Day 7 (70%) CISO / CLO (Day 10) CRO + CFO (Day 14)
Individual Review 5 business days Day 3 (60%) Reviewer's manager (Day 5) Domain head (Day 7)
Governance Review 5 business days Day 3 (60%) CRO (Day 5) COO (Day 7)
Contracting 7 business days Day 5 (70%) CLO / CPO (Day 7) CFO (Day 10)
UAT / Go-Live 5 business days Day 3 (60%) Project Sponsor (Day 5) CTO (Day 7)

Escalation Email Templates

Use these at each escalation level. Be factual, not emotional.

Level 0: Friendly Reminder (at warning threshold)

Subject: Reminder: Review Due [Date] - [Software Name] Hi [Reviewer], Quick reminder that your [domain] review for [Software Name] (ONB-[XXX]) is due in [N] business days ([Date]). If you need additional information or are experiencing any blockers, please let me know so I can help unblock you. Thanks, [Quarterback Name]

Level 1: Formal Escalation (at SLA breach)

Subject: SLA BREACH: [Software Name] - [Phase] overdue by [N] days Hi [Escalation Manager], CC: [Reviewer], [Governance Lead] I'm escalating request ONB-[XXX] ([Software Name]) which has exceeded its SLA in [Phase]. SLA Target: [N] business days Actual Duration: [N] business days (overdue by [N]) Blocker: [Specific reason for delay] Impact: [Business impact of delay] Requester: [Name] ([Department]) Requested action: Please help resolve the blocker or provide an expected completion date by end of business [Date]. Governance Stand-Up: This item will be discussed at the next stand-up on [Date/Time]. [Quarterback Name] Onboarding Facilitator

[Recommended path forward] 2. [Alternative approach] 3. Kill the request (if no longer viable) Your decision is needed by [Date]. [Quarterback Name] Onboarding Facilitator
