3-Pathway Routing Model

Discovery revealed a fundamental third pathway -- Vendor Affinity -- where the organization evaluates and approves but advisors purchase directly. This replaces the binary Buy/Build model.

Buy

Organization purchases licenses, provisions software, bears full cost.

  • Org investment: High
  • Full SP3 evaluation + SP4 contracting
  • Timeline: 19 days (standard)

Build

Internal development with PDLC sub-process. May include third-party components.

  • Org investment: High
  • PDLC lifecycle + integration governance
  • Skips vendor evaluation, enters SP4 directly

Vendor Affinity

Evaluate and approve only -- advisors purchase directly from vendors.

  • Org investment: None/Low
  • Skip financial analysis, compliance review only
  • Timeline: ~30 days (target)

"There's a model where we evaluate it, we approve it, and the advisors go ahead and buy it themselves."

-- Product Manager, describing the Vendor Affinity pathway

DMN-First Decision Intelligence

Every routing decision in the process is driven by a DMN 1.3 decision table -- not hardcoded logic in gateway conditions. This means business rules can be updated without changing the process model.

Why Decision Tables?

  • Auditable: Every decision produces a traceable record
  • Maintainable: Business users can update rules without developer involvement
  • Testable: Each rule can be validated independently
  • Consistent: Same inputs always produce same outputs

How It Works

BPMN Business Rule Tasks invoke DMN tables at decision points. The gateway reads the DMN output variable (e.g., riskTier, selectedPathway) to route the flow. Business logic lives in the table, not the diagram.

  • AI process: 3 separate committees
