Root Cause Analysis

Underlying drivers of process inefficiency

Root CauseDescription
Process Design Sequential review structure forces each function to wait for the previous one. No parallel execution paths exist for independent reviews.
Governance Unclear decision authority across 12+ review stages. Escalation criteria are informal, and approvals are duplicated across committees.
Risk Framework Overly conservative reviews applied uniformly regardless of vendor risk profile. A low-risk SaaS renewal receives the same scrutiny as a critical infrastructure vendor.
Documentation Inconsistent vendor requirements across intake forms and questionnaires. Each function maintains its own templates, creating confusion for both internal teams and vendors.

Future State Design Principles

Five guiding principles for the redesigned onboarding process

01
Risk-Based Onboarding Tiers

Classify vendors by risk profile and apply proportional review depth.

02
Parallel Review Workflows

Execute independent reviews simultaneously instead of sequentially.

03
Simplified Governance Structure

Consolidate approval layers and define clear decision authority at each stage.

04
Standardized Vendor Intake

Unified intake form that collects all required information upfront.

05
Clear Ownership: Quarterback Role

Single point of accountability who shepherds each request through the lifecycle.

Risk-Tiered Vendor Model

Proportional review depth based on vendor risk classification

Vendor TierDescriptionReview LevelSLA
Fast-Track (6-8 pts) Low Risk Quarterback + 1 reviewer 5 days
Standard (9-13 pts) Moderate Risk 4 reviewers in parallel 15 days
Enhanced (14-18 pts) High Risk All reviewers + executive briefing 25 days
This model allows lower-risk vendors to onboard faster while maintaining appropriate oversight for higher-risk engagements. Risk scoring uses a composite of data sensitivity, criticality, regulatory exposure, and vendor maturity.
